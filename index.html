Camel

  1. There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  2. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  3. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.

Chameleon

  1. Chameleons are a very unique branch of the lizard group of reptiles.
  2. There are around 160 species of chameleon.
  3. Chameleons live in warm varied habitats from rainforests through to deserts.

Cicada

  1. Cicadas are like tiny violins.
  2. Cicadas make noise like toy frogs.
  3. They’re underground builders.

Panda

  1. A giant panda is much bigger than your teddy bear.
  2. Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  3. Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).

Pigeon

  1. Size of pigeon depends on the species. Large pigeons can reach 19 inches in length and 8.8 pounds of weight. Small pigeons can reach 5 inches in length and up to 0.8 ounces of weight.
  2. Pigeons can have dull or colorful plumage, depending on the habitat and type of diet. The most common type of pigeon (that lives in the cities) has grayish plumage. On average, pigeon has 10 000 feathers on the body.
  3. Pigeons have strong muscles used for flying. They can fly at the altitude of 6000 feet.

Teddy

  1. The term bear-hug was first recorded in 1846.
  2. The Teddy Bears' Picnic song was originally called The Teddy Bear Two Step.
  3. The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
